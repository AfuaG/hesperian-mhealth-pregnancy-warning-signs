Fever during pregnancy

If a woman has a fever during pregnancy, it could be caused by malaria, a sickness like flu, a bladder or kidney infection, or dehydration. Find the cause of the fever and treat it.

Along with the fever does she also:

  • Have shivering, body aches and severe headache? She might have malaria.
  • Have a cough, stuffy nose, and feel weak in her body? She might have the flu.
  • Feel the need to urinate more often or have pain or burning with urination? She might have a bladder or kidney infection.
  • Feel thirsty, have little or no urine, dry mouth, feel dizzy when standing up, or have loss of stretchiness in her skin? She may be dehydrated. Give rehydration drink.

Along with treating the cause, a high fever needs to be lowered right away.

Bleeding during the first half of pregnancy: miscarriage

Bleeding as much as monthly bleeding during the first half of pregnancy is usually a miscarriage (losing a pregnancy) and is not usually dangerous. But Watch for danger signs like too much bleeding, or signs of infection such as fever, feeling faint, or a bad smell from the vagina.

Women who have had miscarriages or abortions may feel afraid, sad, or upset. This emotional pain is just as important as the pain women have in their bodies.

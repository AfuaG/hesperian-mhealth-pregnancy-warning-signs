Bleeding after Birth

It is normal for a woman to bleed a little after birth. But bleeding is a serious problem if she:

  1. Soaks 2 or more cloths or pads in an hour.
  2. Bleeds more than 2 cups.
  3. Has any signs of losing too much blood: feeling faint, pale skin, feeling like she lost too much blood.
Any of these signs are very dangerous. Take the mother to the hospital right away. Watch her for signs of shock.

If she doesn't have any of the signs above, you can help to stop the bleeding. Has the placenta come out yet?

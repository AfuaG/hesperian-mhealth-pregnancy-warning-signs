How to treat yeast infections

Yeast infections are common during pregnancy. They are not dangerous, but should be treated before birth.

Signs:
  • very itchy inside or outside the vagina
  • white, lumpy discharge, like milk curd or yogurt
  • bright red skin outside and inside the vagina that may bleed
  • a burning feeling when urinating
  • a smell like mold or baking bread
Treatment:
  • Mix 3 tablespoons of vinegar with 1 liter boiled and cooled water. Soak a piece of clean cotton in the mixture and insert it into the vagina every night for 3 nights. Remove the cotton each morning. OR
  • Soak a clean piece of cotton in gentian violet 0.5%. Insert the cotton into the vagina, every night for 3 nights. Remove the cotton each morning. OR
  • Insert 200 mg miconazole high in the vagina each night for 3 nights. OR
  • Insert 100,000 units nystatin high in the vagina each night for 14 nights.

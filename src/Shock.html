Shock

Shock is a life-threatening condition that can result from heavy bleeding. Bleeding inside the body can also cause shock.

Signs:

  • Very fast heart rate, more than 110 beats a minute
  • Pale, cold, damp skin
  • Pale inner eyelids, mouth, and palms
  • Fast breathing, more than 30 breaths a minute
  • Confusion or unconsciousness (fainting)
If she is unconscious, do not wait for a health worker. Take the woman to a hospital right away. She needs medical help fast.

How to treat a woman in shock

  • Lay her down with her head to one side. Put something under her legs to make sure her feet are higher than her head. Cover her with a blanket or clothes. Help her to stay calm.
  • If she has vomited, be sure her head is low, tilted back and to one side, so she does not breathe vomit into her lungs.
  • If she can drink, give her sips of water or rehydration drink. If she cannot drink, start rectal fluids. Or if you know how, start a fast intravenous drip (IV) with a wide needle.

If she is unconscious, do not give her anything by mouth - no medicines, drink or food until she has been awake for one hour.

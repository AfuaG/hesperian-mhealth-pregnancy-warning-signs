How to strengthen labor

Do not use injections to start labor. They can cause contractions that are so strong that they can kill the woman or the baby.

First, check the baby's position to make sure he is not sideways.

  • Have the woman urinate.
  • Roll her nipples. Or have a baby nurse.
  • Mix about ¼ cup castor oil with some juice and have the mother drink it. This usually causes painful diarrhea and cramps, but it may also strengthen contractions.
  • If she has been pushing for a long time, have her change positions. She can try squatting, getting on her hands and knees, or sitting against a pillow with her knees up.
If the baby is still not coming, get medical help. Medication or surgery may be needed.

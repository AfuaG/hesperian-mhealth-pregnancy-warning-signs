Pre-eclampsia (danger of seizures)

Pre-eclampsia is when a woman has:

  • High blood pressure (140/90 or greater).
  • Protein in her urine.

Pre-eclampsia can cause premature birth, bleeding, seizures, or even death for the mother.

If a woman has both of these signs, she already has pre-eclampsia and needs medical help right away.

If she only has high blood pressure, check it often to see if it goes up.

If she has high blood pressure, watch for any of these signs of severe pre-eclampsia:

  • Strong headaches.
  • Vision changes such as spots, blurriness, or double vision.
  • Steady, severe pain in the top of the belly or the right side just under the ribs.
  • Swollen face, or swelling all over when she wakes up.
  • Seizures.

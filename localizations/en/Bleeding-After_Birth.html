Bleeding after birth

It is normal for a woman to bleed a little after birth. But treat immediately for bleeding if she:

  • Soaks 2 or more cloths or pads in an hour, OR
  • Bleeds more than 2 cups
  • Treat bleeding before the placenta
  • Treat bleeding after the placenta

Even if you do not see a lot of blood, watch for these signs: feeling faint, pale skin, feeling like she lost too much blood.

These are signs of shock. Take the mother to the hospital right away. Treat for bleeding and shock on the way.

Fever during pregnancy

If a woman has a fever during pregnancy, it could be caused by malaria, a sickness like flu, a kidney infection, or dehydration. Find the cause of the fever and treat it.

Along with the fever does she also:

  • Have shivering, body aches and severe headache? She might have malaria.
  • Have a cough, stuffy nose, body aches, feel weak and perhaps vomiting? She might have the flu.
  • Have pain in the sides or in the middle of the back, and feel the need to urinate more often or have burning with urination? She might have a kidney infection.
  • Feel thirsty, have little or no urine, have dry mouth, or feel dizzy when standing up? She may be dehydrated. Give rehydration drink.

Along with treating the cause, a high fever needs to be lowered right away.
